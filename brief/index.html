Daily Covid-19 Briefing

Wednesday, January 05, 2022 - Prepared by ALPINE1

As of July 29, 2021 the Province of Alberta has begun reducing the number of Covid-19 tests performed. Caution should be applied when interpreting these daily case and trend values as they may not be reflective of the wider population.

Today 4,768 new cases of Covid-19 were identified in the province. The adjusted net total of cases is 4,752 including changes to previous case numbers between October 21st and January 3rd.

The current 7 day trend of cases is 3,755, an increase of 1.3% from the day before. This time last week trend was 2,318. This is an increase of 62%. The 7 day trend currently sits at 182.8% of the previous peak, which reached 2,054 on May 2nd, 2021.

There are 37,196 active cases within the province, up 2,920 from yesterday. This is an increase of 8.5% in the number of active cases. This time last week there were 4,431 active cases. This is an increase of 739%. The current number of active cases is 11,758 over the previous peak of 25,438 on May 9th, 2021.

  • City Of Red Deer - 491.6 case rate per 100k
  • City Of St. Albert - 1077.7 case rate per 100k

The top hotspot municipalities in the provice by active case rates per 100,000 people are currently:

Currently there are a total of 470 patients in hospital, with 72 in intensive care and 398 in non-intensive care. Total net hospitalizations are up 17 from yesterday, with net 10 ICU patients and net 7 non-ICU patients. This time last week there were 57 patients in ICUs, representing an increase of 26%. What is ALPINE1?

This is the first generation of the Assisted Language Processing & Interpretation News Engine. It is designed to assist in the transformation of quantitative and qualitative data into readable news style copy. It works by interpreting sets of data, and then processes that into human understandable sentences.


The estimated active case rate for each of Alberta Health Zones is based on the population counts provided by Alberta Health on the COVID-19 regional active cases table, and may not match calculations using previous census population counts.
