Daily Covid-19 Briefing

Monday, June 21, 2021 - Prepared by ALPINE1

Today 51 new cases of Covid-19 were identified in the province. The adjusted total of cases is 60 including changes to previous case numbers between December 5th and May 8th.

The current 7 day trend of cases is 80, a decrease of -10.9% from the day before. This time last week trend was 153. This is a decrease of -48%. The 7 day trend currently sits at 3.9% of the previous peak, which reached 2,058 on May 2nd, 2021.

There are 2,003 active cases within the province, down -124 from yesterday. This is a decrease of -5.8% in the number of active cases. This time last week there were 3,089 active cases. This is a decrease of -35%. The current number of active cases is -23,435 under the previous peak of 25,438 on May 9th, 2021.

Currently there are a total of 214 patients in hospital, with 56 in intensive care and 158 in non-intensive care. Total hospitalizations are down -1 from yesterday, with 3 ICU patients and -4 non-ICU patients. This time last week there were 73 patients in ICUs, representing a decrease of -23%.

Yesterday 27,065 doses of vaccine were administered in the province. The total adjusted number of doses administered is 29,026 including changes to previously reported doses between January 5th and June 19th. A total of 3,804,700 doses of vaccine have been administered thus far.

The top hotspot municipalities in the provice by active case rates per 100,000 people are currently:

  • Municipal District Of Bighorn No. 8 - 1943.9 case rate per 100k
  • Municipal District Of Opportunity No. 17 - 1273.3 case rate per 100k
  • Municipal District Of Provost No. 52 - 205.7 case rate per 100k
  • Municipal District Of Pincher Creek No. 9 - 155.8 case rate per 100k
  • Woodlands County - 149.7 case rate per 100k

A total of 2 new deaths were reported in the province today (new deaths and updated date of death information counts may not match):

  • A male, aged 50-59 years old from the Calgary Zone, died on June 17th, 2021

Currently there are 193 schools on 2+ case alert or 5+ case outbreak in the province, up 3 from yesterday. This represents 8.5% of the 2,261 schools that have reported enrollment numbers in the province this year. Between May 7th and May 25th, Kindergarten to Grade 12 students were transitioned to online learning as part of the province's Covid-19 prevention measures.

What is ALPINE1?

This is the first generation of the Assisted Language Processing & Interpretation News Engine. It is designed to assist in the transformation of quantitative and qualitative data into readable news style copy. It works by interpreting sets of data, and then processes that into human understandable sentences.


The estimated active case rate for each of Alberta Health Zones is based on the population counts provided by Alberta Health on the COVID-19 regional active cases table, and may not match calculations using previous census population counts.
