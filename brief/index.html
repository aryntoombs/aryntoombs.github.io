Daily Covid-19 Briefing

Monday, May 10, 2021 - Prepared by ALPINE1

Today 1,601 new cases of Covid-19 were identified in the province. The adjusted total of cases is 1,597 including changes to previous case numbers between November 15th and May 7th.

The current 7 day trend of cases is 1,844, a decrease of -1.6% from the day before. This time last week trend was 2,011. This is a decrease of -8.3%. The 7 day trend currently sits at 106.2% of the previous peak, which reached 1,737 on December 5th, 2020.

There are 25,438 active cases within the province, up 241 from yesterday. This is an increase of 0.96% in the number of active cases. This time last week there were 23,608 active cases. This is an increase of 7.8%. The current number of active cases is 4,315 over the previous peak of 21,123 on December 13th, 2020.

Currently there are a total of 690 patients in hospital, with 158 in intensive care and 532 in non-intensive care. Total hospitalizations are up 15 from yesterday, with 3 ICU patients and 12 non-ICU patients. This time last week there were 153 patients in ICUs, representing an increase of 3.3%.

Yesterday 24,112 doses of vaccine were administered in the province yesterday. The total adjusted number of doses administered is 27,918 including changes to previously reported doses between January 1st and May 8th. A total of 1,916,957 doses of vaccine have been administered thus far.

The top hotspot municipalities in the provice by active case rates per 100,000 people are currently:

  • Lac La Biche - 2033 case rate per 100k
  • Fort Mcmurray - 1864.8 case rate per 100k
  • Regional Municipality Of Wood Buffalo - 1651 case rate per 100k
  • Municipal District Of Bighorn No. 8 - 1413.7 case rate per 100k

A total of 7 new deaths were reported in the province today:

  • A male, aged 60-69 years old from the Edmonton Zone, died on April 15th, 2021
  • A male, aged 70-79 years old from the Calgary Zone, died on April 27th, 2021
  • A female, aged 70-79 years old from the Central Zone, died on April 28th, 2021
  • A female, aged 80+ years old from the Edmonton Zone, died on April 29th, 2021
  • A male, aged 80+ years old from the South Zone, died on April 30th, 2021
  • A male, aged 80+ years old from the Central Zone, died on May 4th, 2021
  • A female, aged 40-49 years old from the Edmonton Zone, died on May 7th, 2021

Currently there are 793 schools on alert or outbreak in the province, up 65 from yesterday. This represents 35.1% of the 2,261 schools that have reported enrollment numbers in the province this year. As of May 7th, Kindergarten to Grade 12 students will transition to online learning until May 25th as part of the province's Covid-19 prevention measures.

What is ALPINE1?

This is the first generation of the Assisted Language Processing & Interpretation News Engine. It is designed to assist in the transformation of quantitative and qualitative data into readable news style copy. It works by interpreting sets of data, and then processes that into human understandable sentences.
