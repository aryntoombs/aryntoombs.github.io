Daily Covid-19 Briefing

Thursday, March 03, 2022 - Prepared by ALPINE1

As of July 29, 2021 the Province of Alberta has begun reducing the number of Covid-19 tests performed. Caution should be applied when interpreting these daily case and trend values as they may not be reflective of the wider population.

Today 634 new cases of Covid-19 were identified in the province. The adjusted net total of cases is 539 including changes to previous case numbers between December 21st and March 1st.

The current 7 day trend of cases is 500, a decrease of -2.7% from the day before. This time last week trend was 578. This is a decrease of -13%. The 7 day trend currently sits at 8.1% of the previous peak, which reached 6,165 on January 10th, 2022.

There are 7,945 active cases within the province, down -219 from yesterday. This is a decrease of -2.7% in the number of active cases. This time last week there were 16,551 active cases. This is a decrease of -52%. The current number of active cases is -62,278 under the previous peak of 70,223 on January 17th, 2022.

Currently there are a total of 1,204 patients in hospital, with 80 in intensive care and 1,124 in non-intensive care. Total net hospitalizations are down -45 from yesterday, with net -4 ICU patients and net -41 non-ICU patients. This time last week there were 93 patients in ICUs, representing a decrease of -14%. What is ALPINE1?

This is the first generation of the Assisted Language Processing & Interpretation News Engine. It is designed to assist in the transformation of quantitative and qualitative data into readable news style copy. It works by interpreting sets of data, and then processes that into human understandable sentences.


The estimated active case rate for each of Alberta Health Zones is based on the population counts provided by Alberta Health on the COVID-19 regional active cases table, and may not match calculations using previous census population counts.
